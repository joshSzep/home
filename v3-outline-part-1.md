## Chapter 1: Detection
**POV: Amara Okafor (52)**  
**Date: Year 1, Day 1 (January 15, 3025)**  
**Word Count Target: 4,500**

### Opening
- 3:47 AM at Antarctic Monitoring Station
- Amara alone on graveyard shift—this is her preference
- Twenty years of work, comfortable isolation
- The station's atmosphere: quiet hum, screens glowing, tea cooling
- Reviewing routine data on asteroid 2024-KJ9

### The Detection
- High-priority alert (rare—maybe three times in twenty years)
- New object detected: unusual signature
- Initial data: mass ~1.5 × 10^15 kg, 10-15 km diameter
- Velocity: 28.3 km/s relative to Earth
- Impact probability climbing: 94.3%... 96.1%... 98.7%...

### Verification
- Amara runs protocols (show her expertise, precision)
- Checks for system errors: none
- Pulls raw telescope data from three monitoring stations
- Parallax measurements, spectral analysis, brightness curve
- Manual calculation confirms AI assessment
- Impact probability: 99.97%
- Time to impact: 15 years, 2 months, 14 days

### The Moment of Understanding
- Amara's hands shaking
- The tea getting cold
- Opening deflection calculator
- Required delta-v: 0.3 cm/s, but needed years ago
- Running every deflection scenario:
  - Kinetic impactor: spacecraft too massive to build
  - Nuclear deflection: insufficient yield
  - Gravity tractor: needs 50+ years minimum
- All scenarios: FAILURE
- The physics is absolute
- No solutions exist

### Protocol and Isolation
- Emergency notification protocols activate
- Calls to UN coordinator, space agencies, government heads
- Amara delivers the data clinically, precisely
- They ask: "Are you certain?" She answers: "Yes."
- The calls end. The protocols complete.
- Amara sits in the silent station

### The Personal Cost
- She should call her sister Zara (they haven't spoken in six months)
- Their estrangement: unspoken resentment, different paths
- Amara chose career over family, Antarctica over Lagos
- Now: fifteen years until everyone dies
- Including Zara. Including little Maya (who Amara barely knows)
- She picks up the comm unit
- Stares at Zara's contact
- Can't press call
- Sets it down
- Returns to the data
- Updates the countdown: 15 years, 2 months, 14 days

### Closing
- The screens glow in the darkness
- The asteroid approaches at 28.3 km/s
- Amara sits alone
- First human to know: everyone is dying
- The weight of that knowledge
- The isolation she chose
- Now permanent

### Key Elements to Establish
- Amara's competence and precision
- Her isolation as choice, not circumstance
- The estrangement from Zara (hints of history)
- Antarctica setting: cold, beautiful, remote
- The asteroid as physics, not malice
- The certainty of the math
- Amara's defense mechanism: calculation without feeling

## Chapter 3: Crisis of Faith (Part 1)
**POV: Ibrahim al-Rashid (19)**  
**Date: Year 1, Day 1 (January 15, 3025) — Evening**  
**Word Count Target: 3,800**

### Opening: Evening Prayers
- Jerusalem hills, militia camp
- Ibrahim kneeling with brothers on prayer mats
- Facing Mecca, forehead to ground
- *Allahu Akbar* — God is greatest
- The words automatic after lifetime of practice
- But they still mean everything
- Foundation. Purpose. Certainty.

### Ibrahim's Faith (Before)
- Raised in the faith, devout family
- Joined militia at 16 (defending holy land)
- Jerusalem: contested for three millennia, still contested
- Forever Earth made Earth permanent home
- Made Jerusalem permanently significant
- Ibrahim's certainty: this land is sacred, this fight righteous
- Martyrdom leads to paradise
- Death in holy war is honor
- The faith is complete, total, unquestioned

### The Interruption
- Commander's voice: "Brothers. Emergency broadcast. Everyone to command tent. Now."
- Prayers interrupted (unusual, significant)
- The militia gathering: 150 fighters, all devout
- Screen displaying UN broadcast
- Asteroid. Extinction. Fifteen years.
- Impact probability: 99.97%

### The Breaking
- Imam speaking: "Brothers, this is test. Allah's will."
- Others nodding, accepting
- "The faithful will be rewarded in paradise."
- "This world was always temporary. The next is eternal."
- Ibrahim listening, trying to feel the certainty
- But: a crack forming
- Small at first, then widening

### The Question
- If everyone dies—Muslim, Christian, Jew, atheist—what's the difference?
- The holy land they fight for: vaporized
- The sacred sites: destroyed
- Jerusalem, Mecca, all of it: physics
- If this is Allah's plan, what are they? Puppets?
- The theological framework that held everything: wobbling

### After Prayers
- Others return to normal activities (as if nothing changed)
- How can they accept so easily?
- Ibrahim's commander: "We fight until the end. The mission continues."
- "What mission?" Ibrahim asks. "The land we're defending will be destroyed."
- Commander: "Then we defend it until it is. That's faith."
- Ibrahim: "That's not faith. That's denial."
- The commander's face hardens. "Watch your words, brother."

### Alone with the Beads
- Ibrahim's prayer beads (*misbaha*): 99 beads for 99 names of God
- Held them since childhood
- Worn them in combat
- Prayed with them after every raid
- Now: they feel like stones
- Just weight. Just objects.
- The prayers just words without meaning

### The Imam's Visit
- Old imam sitting beside him (uninvited but welcome)
- "Brother Ibrahim, you're troubled."
- "I'm questioning."
- "Questions are good. But don't let the asteroid steal your faith. That's death before death."
- "What if faith doesn't make sense anymore?"
- "Faith isn't about sense. It's about surrender to Allah's will."
- "What if I can't surrender to this?"

### Closing
- Ibrahim alone in his tent
- Prayer beads in hand
- Trying to pray, can't find the words
- The crack in his faith widening
- He fights for sacred land that won't exist
- He believes in paradise he's no longer certain of
- He serves Allah whose plan seems cruel
- First night: faith breaking
- Fifteen years ahead: faith gone entirely
- He doesn't know that yet
- Still trying to pray
- The beads feeling like stones

### Key Elements to Establish
- Ibrahim's deep, sincere faith (make it real, not straw man)
- Jerusalem context: why this fight matters to him
- The militia as community, not just fighters
- His youth (19) and idealism
- The crack in faith beginning (not instant atheism)
- Islamic practice shown respectfully, accurately
- The tension: surrender to God's will vs. the will seeming cruel
- Setup for his journey from faith to presence without belief

### Closing

- The gardenias blooming
- The lemon tree heavy with fruit
- Antarctica sunlight (engineered warm)

- Everything beautiful
- Everything here now
