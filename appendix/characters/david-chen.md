# David Chen - THE BUILDER

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** David Chen  
**Age at Detection:** 49 (born 2976)  
**Age at Impact:** 64 (dies 3040)  
**Location:** Antarctica Megacity (Infrastructure Division)  
**Ancestry:** Chinese (Han) ancestry, family emigrated to Antarctica three generations ago  
**Occupation:** Senior Infrastructure Engineer, Solar Array Maintenance Division

---

## Physical Description

- Tall (183cm/6')
- Chinese features
- Graying black hair (fully gray by Year 15)
- Weathered from outdoor work
- Calloused hands
- Quiet eyes, observant
- Ages visibly over 15 years: thinner, gray, weathered, but still strong
- Moves deliberately, economically

---

## Background

### Early Life (2976-2998)
- Born in Antarctica (third generation)
- Parents: both infrastructure engineers
- Grandparents emigrated during Early Emigration (2900s)
- Grew up on maintenance schedules
- Learned to fix things before learned to read
- Quiet child, preferred machines to people
- But not antisocial - just comfortable with silence

### Education & Career (2998-3010)
- Studied infrastructure engineering
- Specialized in solar arrays
- Good with his hands
- Practical, methodical
- Joined Solar Array Division at 22
- Steady, reliable, promoted to senior engineer

### Meeting Amara (3010)
- Met Dr. Amara Okafor at facility
- She: analyzing asteroid threats (theoretical then)
- He: maintaining solar panels
- She talked (a lot), he listened
- She: brilliant, intense, passionate
- He: steady, grounded, calm
- Opposites attracted
- She said: "You're the only person who makes me slow down."
- He said: "You're the only person who makes me talk."

### Marriage & Life Pre-Detection (3012-3025)
- Married 3012 (he was 36, she was 40)
- No children (her choice - career focus, he accepted)
- Good marriage initially
- Then: she became consumed by work
- He: gave her space, maybe too much
- Drifted apart slowly
- Still married, still living together
- But disconnected
- He buried himself in work
- Routine, maintenance, reliability
- What they had: familiar, comfortable, distant

## Personality & Worldview (Pre-Detection)

### Core Traits
- **Quiet:** Comfortable with silence
- **Reliable:** Shows up, does the work
- **Practical:** Solves problems with hands
- **Patient:** Can wait, can endure
- **Stoic:** Doesn't express emotion easily (but feels deeply)

### Beliefs
- **Meaning through work:** Building, maintaining, repairing
- **Systems matter:** Infrastructure is civilization
- **Stability is good:** Predictability, routine, order
- **Less is more:** Simple life, simple needs
- **Actions over words:** Show up, do the work

### Strengths
- Extremely reliable
- Skilled with hands
- Patient, enduring
- Observant (notices details)
- Steady in crisis

### Weaknesses
- Emotionally withdrawn
- Avoids confrontation
- Can be passive
- Struggles to express feelings
- Can seem cold (he's not - just quiet)

## The Detection Moment (3025)

### Where He Was
- On solar array platform, routine maintenance
- 200 meters up, alone
- Radio broadcast interrupts: AI alert
- Listens to whole thing
- Asteroid, unstoppable, 15 years
- Stands there, looking at sky
- Can't see asteroid (daylight, too small)
- But knows it's there

### What He Understood
- Everything I maintain: for nothing
- These solar panels: will function until impact
- This city: will exist 15 years, then vapor
- Work: meaningless
- Life: ending
- Amara: we wasted so much time

### Immediate Response
- Finishes maintenance task (habit, training)
- Climbs down slowly
- Goes home
- Amara not there (she's at work, analyzing data)
- He waits
- She comes home hours later: devastated, numb
- He: "Are you okay?"
- She: "No."
- He holds her
- They don't talk much
- Just hold each other
- First real connection in months

### Emotional State
- Shock, numbness
- Grief for wasted time with Amara
- Existential: what was the point of all this work?
- But also: strange relief
- No more endless future
- Just: 15 years
- Manageable
- Can focus on what matters
- But first: collapse

## Character Arc: 15 Years

### Year 1 (3025-3026): THE COLLAPSE
**Internal State:**
- Why maintain anything?
- Existential paralysis
- Goes to work from habit
- But meaningless
- Depression (quiet, unspoken)
- Watching Amara spiral too
- Both in same house, both drowning
- Not connecting

**Actions:**
- Still going to work
- Maintenance division chaos
- Half the workers quit
- Some suicide
- David: shows up, does tasks mechanically
- Supervisor: "Why are you still here?"
- David: "I don't know."
- But keeps showing up
- Home: silent
- Amara working 16 hours/day (avoiding)
- David alone
- They barely speak

**Relationships:**
- Marriage: worse than before
- Amara: consumed by trying to solve unsolvable
- David: letting her go, not fighting
- Coworkers: many leaving, dying
- He: stays, but disconnected

**Key Moment:**
- Day at work, alone on platform
- Thinks: I could jump. Just step off.
- Stands at edge
- Looks down
- Then: decides not to
- Not from hope
- From: what's the difference? Jump now, die in 15 years.
- Might as well keep going.
- Not suicide: passive continuation

**State at Year End:**
- Depressed, numb
- Marriage hollow
- Work meaningless
- But still showing up
- Waiting

### Year 5 (3030): THE REALIZATION
**Internal State:**
- Five years gone
- Still maintaining solar arrays
- Why?
- Interesting discovery: they still work
- Automated systems still running
- AI maintenance protocols: continuing
- They don't know world is ending
- Or they don't care
- Just: maintain function

**Actions:**
- Working with automated systems
- Fewer human workers now
- David and machines
- He notices: machines are better colleagues
- No despair, no meaning-making
- Just: function
- He finds strange peace in this
- Maintaining solar arrays with robots
- The work matters to the system
- Even if it doesn't matter cosmically

**Relationships:**
- Marriage: dead but still cohabiting
- Amara: hollow, going through motions
- They're roommates
- Polite, distant
- David: not fighting it anymore
- Has accepted it

**Key Moment:**
- Repairing array with robotic assistant
- Robot executing maintenance protocol perfectly
- Doesn't care why
- Just: function requires maintenance
- David: "You're right. It doesn't need a reason."
- Continues work
- Strange peace in meaningless function

**State at Year End:**
- Functioning but not living
- Work for work's sake
- Marriage: over in all but name
- Has forgotten what connection feels like

### Year 10 (3035): THE RECONNECTION
**Internal State:**
- Ten years gone
- Five left
- Amara reaches out (finally)
- Says: "We wasted ten years. I don't want to waste five more."
- David: shocked, wary
- But: realizes he doesn't want to die alone
- Doesn't want to die estranged
- Opens up (terrifying for him)

**Actions:**
- Starts actually talking to Amara
- Tells her about robot colleagues
- She listens (really listens, first time in decade)
- They start spending time together
- Not romance (not at first)
- Just: presence
- Coffee in morning
- Meals together
- Walking in preserves
- Quiet connection

**Relationships:**
- **Marriage: rekindling**
- Slow, careful
- Learning each other again
- Different now (both changed)
- But: foundation still there
- Zara (Amara's sister) calls: planning Year 15 visit
- David: "I'd like to meet your family properly."
- Amara: grateful

**Key Moment:**
- On solar array platform together
- Amara came up to see his work
- She: "I forgot how beautiful it is up here."
- He: "I stopped noticing."
- She: "I'm sorry I left you alone."
- He: "I let you."
- She: "Can we not be alone anymore?"
- He: "Yes. I'd like that."
- Hold hands, 200 meters up, five years left

**State at Year End:**
- Connected again (tentatively)
- Marriage: healing
- Still doing work (but now with purpose: connection)
- Hopeful (first time in decade)

### Year 15 (3040): THE WITNESS

## Thematic Resonance

### What He Represents
- **Work without future**
- Maintenance that ends anyway
- Function vs. meaning
- Connection vs. isolation
- Quiet endurance
- Late redemption

### His Journey
- From connected to isolated
- From meaning through work to meaningless function
- From closed to open (slowly)
- From alone to together
- **Impermanence: systems outlast us by seconds, but connection makes those seconds matter**

### His Answer
- Work continues until it doesn't
- Systems don't care, but we do
- Connection is what makes us human
- Better late than never
- Witness together

**Breaks:** Years 1-10 depressed, hollow, functioning without living  
**Transforms:** Year 10 reopens, reconnects, learns to be present

**Core Question:** What's the point of maintaining systems that will end anyway?

- Contrast to Amara (married opposites)

- Reconnection Year 10-15: tender, slow, earned

- Work scenes: meditative, repetitive, finding beauty in function

- Represents stoic answer: endure, be present when you can, connection redeems
