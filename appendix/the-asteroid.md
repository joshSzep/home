## The Fundamental Reality

### It Cannot Be Stopped

**This is absolutely certain. Not a question of effort or will. Impossible.**

**Why deflection is impossible:**

1. **Scale**
   - Mass is too enormous
   - Would need to change velocity significantly
   - Physics of momentum: mass Ã— velocity
   - Would take energy we cannot generate

2. **No Fusion Energy**
   - Deflection would require stellar-scale energy
   - We only have solar power
   - Not enough, not even close
   - Even if we had fusion, might not be enough

3. **Velocity**
   - Moving incredibly fast
   - Kinetic energy scales with velocity squared
   - Interstellar speeds = unstoppable

4. **Time**
   - 15 years is not enough
   - To build the systems we'd need
   - To position them
   - To execute deflection over years
   - Even if we had the capability (we don't)

5. **Physics is Physics**
   - This is like the light speed barrier
   - This is like aging
   - This is like fusion
   - **Another limit we cannot overcome**
   - The universe says no

**Even if humanity united perfectly:**
- Pooled all resources
- Ended all wars
- Focused entirely on deflection
- Built everything we could
- Worked together flawlessly
- **Still couldn't stop it**

**We are not different beings. We cannot overcome this.**

**Leaving Earth is still impossible.**

**Even facing extinction, physics hasn't changed:**
- Light speed still insurmountable
- Nearest star still 4.24 light-years away

**15 years is not enough to:**
- Develop new physics (there isn't any)
- Build viable interstellar ships (impossible anyway)
- Evacuate even a tiny fraction of 12 billion
- Reach anywhere safe (nearest star takes centuries)

**Even if we tried:**
- Who goes? Who decides?
- Lottery? Merit? Wealth?
- Genetic haves vs. have-nots?
- Fight over limited spots
- Ships probably fail anyway
- **Human nature prevents even attempting this**

**The Five Prisons hold even now:**
- Physics locks us on Earth
- Biology locks us in time (15 years = some will die of old age before impact)
- AI limits lock us in flesh
- Energy locks us to the sun
- Human nature locks us in patterns

**We cannot leave. We cannot stop it. We simply end.**

### The 15 Years

**Story probably won't cover all 15 years linearly.**

- **Focus on first year** (immediate reactions, adjustment, establishing new normal)

- **Denial:** "They must be wrong"
- **Panic:** Mass hysteria, riots
- **Despair:** Suicide rates spike
- **Anger:** At whom? God? Universe? Scientists? Government?
- **Numbness:** Can't process it

**War Never Changes**
- **New wars ignite** - particularly among theological remnants
- **Control of holy lands = highest priority** for those consumed by such things
- End of Days prophecies drive violence
- Equatorial wars escalate dramatically

**Demographics Shift**
- **Birth rates plummet** (why bring children into doomed world?)
- **Suicide rates skyrocket** (can't face 15 years of waiting)
- Mass grief, mass despair

**Economic Near-Collapse**
- **Economy nearly completely collapses**
- Why work for future that doesn't exist?
- Some sectors boom (experiences, hedonism)
- Others crash (construction, long-term planning)

**Society Has Fractured**
- No unified response
- Humanity splits into factions, each with own way of facing end
- Intentional communities, deniers, builders, abandoned

**Antarctica Status**
- **Holding itself together with string and duct tape**
- Mayor trying to govern but authority questionable
- Some districts function, others anarchic
- Infrastructure degrading
- Corporations lost most power

**Equatorial Wars**
- **Have escalated dramatically**
- No reason to hold back
- Desperation and fanaticism combined
- Death toll meaningless (everyone dies anyway)

### Year 10 (3035)

**Civilization Barely Recognizable**

**Final Days**

- No unified response even now
- Some together, some alone
- Some fighting, some creating, some caring
- Some celebrating, some despairing

### The Philosophical Answer

**IMPERMANENCE**

Drawing from Buddhism and Humanism:

- **This is what we always faced**
- Death was always certain
- For individuals, now for species
- Nothing lasts - this is the fundamental truth
- **Every moment is precious BECAUSE nothing lasts**
- Impermanence is not tragedy, it's reality
- We find meaning IN the transience, not despite it

**The difference between defiance and acceptance:**
- There is no difference
- To accept impermanence IS to defy meaninglessness
- To live fully knowing death IS both acceptance and defiance
- **We matter because we don't last**

**What this means for characters:**
- Some learn this quickly
- Some never learn it
- Some knew it already
- Story shows different relationships to impermanence
- All paths valid
- All paths lead to same ending
