# REVISION.md — Editorial Revision Tracking

## Overview

This document tracks the revision pass following the editorial letter. The goal is **refinement, not rewrite**: compression of the middle, expansion of convergence, and polish throughout.

---

## Core Diagnosis

### What's Working
- Emotional and philosophical depth
- Clear thematic spine (presence, maintenance, community, isolation)
- Exceptional set-piece scenes
- Restraint and trust in the reader
- Multi-POV execution (8 POVs, none irrelevant)

### What Needs Work
1. **Part II is ~15% too long** — repetitive internal reflection, stasis
2. **POV voices too similar** — especially Zara, David, Wei, Soso, early Maya
3. **Convergence too fast** — fracture 300pp, reunion ~40pp
4. **David Chen underwritten** — crucial but least explored
5. **Lagos needs sensory grounding** — impressionistic vs. Antarctica's vividness
6. **Prose cadence repetition** — overuse of clipped sentences and identical chapter buttons

## DETAILED VOICE ANALYSIS

### ZARA OKAFOR-MENSAH

**Current Voice Patterns:**
- Engineering mind that processes emotionally ("Her engineering mind engaged automatically, calculating before her emotional mind could catch up")
- Observational, functional narration of domestic scenes
- Short declarative sentences, same clipped rhythm as Amara
- Introspective but functional—she reflects, then acts
- "Gift work" language from Wei integrated into her worldview

**Problem:**
Zara reads as a slightly warmer version of Amara. Both use:
- Technical/mathematical framing for emotional content
- Same sentence rhythm (short. declarative. pause. emotional beat.)
- Similar internal monologue structure
- The counting/calculating habit

**Distinctiveness Needed:**
Zara is an *engineer* and a *mother*. Her voice should reflect:
1. **Practical physicality** — she builds, touches, fixes things. More sensory detail about hands, materials, textures.
2. **Warmth through action** — she shows love through doing (making breakfast, designing water systems, tending children). Less internal processing, more physical engagement.
3. **Community orientation** — Zara thinks in systems of people, not just data. She sees networks, relationships, interconnections.
4. **Lagos grounding** — heat, humidity, color, sound. Her environment should press into her awareness.
5. **Engineer's pragmatism** — "What needs doing now?" instead of philosophical abstraction.

**Concrete Revisions:**
- Add sensory Lagos details: the smell of synthetic cooking, the humidity on skin, the sound of neighbors
- Replace internal monologue with action—she processes BY doing, not before doing
- More dialogue, less rumination
- Warmth through touch: holding children, touching Kwame, physical presence
- Less calculation language, more building/constructing metaphors

### DAVID CHEN

**Current Voice Patterns:**
- Maintenance as metaphor (strong and should remain)
- Partnership with 47B (distinct and excellent)
- Long internal monologues about marriage, loss, isolation
- Repetitive structure: work detail, memory, return to work
- Same clipped sentence rhythm as other POVs
- Reflections on Amara that feel abstract rather than visceral

**Problem:**
David's interiority is *told* rather than *shown*. We're told he's sad about Amara, but we don't feel it in his body. His maintenance work is beautifully rendered, but his emotional life is generic.

**Distinctiveness Needed:**
David is a *maintenance worker*—his mind should work like his hands: methodical, physical, practical.
1. **Embodied presence** — he notices physical details, textures, the feel of tools
2. **Partnership over isolation** — his relationship with 47B is the model; he connects through work, not words
3. **Quiet grief** — less internal monologue about feelings, more physical manifestation (tight shoulders, cold tea, not eating)
4. **Competence as identity** — he knows his value through doing well, not thinking deeply
5. **Morning ritual** — 6:47 AM is sacred; the routine IS his stability

**Concrete Revisions:**
- Cut internal monologue by 30-40%, replace with physical detail
- Show marriage breakdown through sensory absence: cold bed, untouched dinners, silent rooms
- More dialogue (even with robot)—David thinks through conversation, not reflection
- Physical sensation: the cold, the wind, the vibration of arrays, the weight of tools
- Less meta-commentary on "maintenance as meditation"—just show it

### PROFESSOR MEI-LIN WEI

**Current Voice Patterns:**
- Calm, measured, philosophical
- Garden observation as teaching method
- Age-awareness (114 years, shaking hands, wheelchair)
- Buddhist concepts delivered clearly but sometimes generically
- Patience, stillness, acceptance as dominant mode

**Problem:**
Wei feels like a wisdom-dispensing function rather than a fully embodied person. Her philosophical observations are clear but occasionally read like fortune cookies. She lacks:
- Personal history beyond brief Beijing mentions
- Humor (she's 114—she's seen everything, should have some ironic distance)
- Physical specificity of *her* body, not just "old body"
- Emotional range—she's always calm, always teaching

**Distinctiveness Needed:**
Wei is 114 years old, from Beijing, lived through enormous historical change. She should sound like:
1. **Ancient and specific** — particular memories, particular losses, particular joys
2. **Dry humor** — she's earned the right to be wry about human folly
3. **Sensory memory** — her grandmother's garden in Beijing, specific scents, specific textures
4. **Physical intimacy with aging** — not generic "my body is old" but specific complaints, specific accommodations
5. **Teaching through silence** — less explaining, more demonstrating

**Concrete Revisions:**
- Add one flashback or detailed memory scene (Beijing garden, dead husband, specific teaching moment)
- Inject quiet humor: wry observations about human pretense, gentle irony
- Make her physical experience specific: which joints hurt, how she adjusts, what exhausts her
- Less direct philosophy, more Socratic—let her ask questions rather than give answers
- Show her limits: moments of impatience, tiredness, doubt

## PROSE PATTERN ISSUES

**Overused Cadence:**
```
Short sentence.
Another short sentence.
Emotional beat.
"Enough."
```

This appears in nearly every chapter ending. Examples:
- "Just: alive." (Ibrahim)
- "That was enough." (Zara)
- "Enough." (multiple)
- "Everything continuing. Everything temporary. Everything beautiful." (Wei)
- "Just: here. Now. Real." (David)

**Fix:** Vary chapter endings. Let some chapters end mid-action. Let some trail off. Let some build to longer sentences. The staccato works when used sparingly—currently overused by 300%.

**Sentence Length Pattern:**
Most paragraphs follow: medium-short-short-short-long. This creates hypnotic rhythm but flattens emotional peaks. 

**Fix:** Map high-emotional moments and expand sentence length there. Let grief and love breathe in longer, more complex sentences. Save staccato for numbness and routine.
